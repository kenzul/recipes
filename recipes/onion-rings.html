Onion Rings

Description

These crispy, golden onion rings are the perfect snack or side dish. Made with sweet onions and a seasoned batter, they’re deep-fried to perfection for a satisfying crunch and delicious flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 large onions (yellow or sweet), sliced into 1/4-inch rings
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 cup cold sparkling water (or cold beer)
  • Oil for frying (vegetable or canola)

Steps

  1. Separate the sliced onion rings and soak them in cold water for 30 minutes. Drain and pat dry.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, cornstarch, baking powder, salt, garlic powder, and paprika.
  3. Gradually add the cold sparkling water (or beer) to the dry ingredients, whisking until smooth.
  4. Heat oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 350°F (175°C).
  5. Dip each onion ring into the batter, allowing excess to drip off.
  6. Carefully place the battered rings into the hot oil in batches. Fry for 2–3 minutes or until golden brown, turning occasionally.
  7. Remove the onion rings with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
  8. Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce.
